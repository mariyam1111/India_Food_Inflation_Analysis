# India Food CPI Analysis (2-Year Data)

## Overview
This project analyzes the **Consumer Price Index (CPI) of major food categories in India** over a 2-year period.  
The aim is to **visualize trends, identify high-inflation categories, and generate actionable insights** for businesses and policymakers.  

The project demonstrates professional **data cleaning, visualization, and analysis skills** using Python.

---

## Dataset
- **Source:** Kaggle - [Consumer Food Price Index India](https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/mrmorj/consumer-food-price-index-india/data)  
- **Time Period:** 2 Years (filtered from full dataset)  
- **Columns Analyzed:**  
  - Cereals and products  
  - Meat and fish  
  - Egg  
  - Milk and products  
  - Oils and fats  
  - Fruits  
  - Vegetables  
  - Pulses and products  
  - Sugar and Confectionery  
  - Spices  

---

## Key Features / Skills Demonstrated
- **Data Cleaning:** Fixed typos, stripped spaces, converted months to proper datetime, handled missing values.  
- **Data Analysis:** Calculated month-over-month % changes and volatility for each category.  
- **Data Visualization:**  
  - Line charts showing trends of all categories  
  - Bar charts for average CPI per category  
  - Heatmaps for visualizing spikes and seasonal patterns  
  - Subplots for detailed category-wise trends  
- **Insights Generation:** Identified high-inflation and high-volatility food categories.  

---

## Key Insights

1. **Highest Inflation Categories:**  
   - Vegetables, Oils and fats, and Milk and products show the highest CPI trends.  

2. **Seasonal Patterns:**  
   - Monthly spikes are visible in vegetables and fruits during summer months.  

3. **Volatility Analysis:**  
   - Categories prone to sharp month-over-month price changes: Vegetables, Milk and products, Spices.  

4. **Business Implications:**  
   - Retailers and FMCG companies can adjust pricing and inventory strategies based on high-volatility categories.  

5. **Government / Policy Implications:**  
   - Policymakers can use this analysis to implement **subsidies, price stabilization, and buffer-stock planning** for essential food items.
